Design Animation Studies // 7 November

I made the combat droid key art into a 2D rig so I could make sure everything works prior to modeling it and figuring out much later that the design isn't working. So far I notice the range of motion on the feet isn't all too good, and I need a wrist joint for the hands (which are invisibly rigged like arms).

A fast run test. The character doesn't do much running in the film but I thought it to be a better range of motion test than a walk.

Straight upward jump. This machine has a lot of power but also a lot of weight, it's agile but in a heavy sort of way. The issues with the feet are very visible here. The physics on the tail have an issue on the way down which I couldn't iron out, and the energy frill disappears improperly.

Treatment Beta + Logline // 16 September

Taking my Story Ladder and clumping it together into one blob of text:

A combat droid attempts to make peace with a robot it had destroyed, but the reactivated robot still holds a grudge.

The combat droid revisits a devastated portion of the city. Out of the wreckage, it finds a dilapidated small robot, which it fixes up a bit and reactivates. The small robot thanks the one who fixed it, but upon identifying the combat droid, its demeanor changes towards something much more violent. The small robot goes into offensive mode and attacks the combat droid. It refuses to fight back. The small robot insists it will destroy the combat droid, it will tear its superiors to the ground, it made a grave mistake in reactivating it. Still the combat droid refuses to fight. The small robot starts to question its actions. The combat droid insists that it is sorry for its actions, that it was following orders it should have refused. It was wrong for it to deactivate the small robot and everything else in this war zone, it should have offered peace, and while it doesn’t expect forgiveness it hopes to do whatever it can to make up for it, before its superiors discover its treason. With enough insistence, the small robot reverts to its normal form. The small robot cannot understand how the combat droid can defy its programming in this way. Its niceness does not compute. It apologizes for attacking the combat droid and runs away. The combat droid does not pursue it.

Ideation // 15 September

The core of this film will be a heartfelt story about choosing nonviolence and making amends; but judge it from the cover and it's COOL FUNNY NEON ROBOT STORY.

Idea board, focusing on vibes and character design ideas for the antagonist (this image is huge and I am not sorry)
Main image sources: oxboxer, Rob Sheridan, The Art Of Sonic Frontiers, PrinceWhateverer, Deltarune, myself

I want this to be my first 3D production to have a real landscape to it, and with that I want to accomplish two things: make it striking and keep it simple. A cyberpunk city is a great setting for that, since all it needs to be is a blocky lookin city with lots of neon accents. I've drawn them in the past where buildings are just solid blocks with neon stripes, and that's good enough for me. Should be easy enough for me to figure out, despite the larger scope of the idea.

As for characters, I want to use this fresh new design for the main character. I find robotic bits very easy to model and texture, and the joints (and lack thereof) should be easy enough to rig (the part of the process I struggle the most with). As for the small robot, I want it to have an almost cute, wholesome looking exterior, probably round with a large visor for 'eyes' like WALL-E's EVE, with the capability to suddenly shift into something much more menacing. The top right of the above board is various inspirations I'll likely draw upon when designing that character.

Another huge visual inspiration which you did not expect is this (hilariously terrible) music video for Timewave: Zero by Grendel:


With my last film, I feel I went a bit too small in terms of scope. This time I want to go bigger. I think the story is succinct enough that it's not too big, but the worldbuilding is enough to make it worthwhile. It's gonna be kinda nuts and I'm looking forward to it

Basic Idea

A combat droid discovers and reactivates a small robot, which immediately turns on it for having been the one who deactivated it in the past. However, the combat droid refuses to fight back, instead apologising for not offering the small robot a peaceful path.
