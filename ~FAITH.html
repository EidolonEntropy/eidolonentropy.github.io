FAITH - 72 Hour Dark Love Palette MAP

About

This MAP is about dark love - the characters who express their love in immoral ways. Perhaps they enchanted their love to love them back. Or perhaps they kill to unite lovers in the afterlife. If it's twisted, this MAP is for them.

This is an anything MAP in the sense that canon characters, AU characters, and OCs can all be featured, so long as they follow the theme. When asking for a part, you must say what character(s) you'll be using, and if it's an OC, please explain them a bit.

Character Examples
  • Yoshikage Kira (Jojo's Bizarre Adventure) - a serial killer who targeted women, treating their severed hands as his girlfriends
  • Doug Rattmann (Portal) - a schizophrenic who is obsessed with his Companion Cubes, which he hallucinates as sentient consciousnesses.
  • Darkstalker (Wings of Fire) - put spells on his soulmate to keep her happy while he pursued his evil goals
  • Anakin Skywalker (Star Wars) - need I even explain?
  • Cat Blanc (Miraculous: Tales of Ladybug and Cat Noir) - heartbreak at its finest, wants to use the Miraculous to grant his wish of life alongside Ladybug

These examples are all male characters but female characters work too.

This is a palette MAP designed to draw attention to blood and gore, so if applicable, please include this in your part. However, if your character doesn't exhibit bloody behaviors, don't force them to.

As the MAP goes on, the theme should get darker. In the beginning, it should be more lighthearted, taking the song literally, but over time the darkness begins to show. Hence, bloodier parts should be placed later in the MAP. Keep this in mind when choosing characters and parts.

This MAP is primarily PMV due to the time restriction. The pacing should be slow to match the song. Slowness can also be used to instill unease; it's a slow ballad about love, any form of it, even those that aren't quite right.

Technical Aspects

Animation Style

Since this is a 72 hour MAP, please add as much detail as you deem feasible to complete within the time limit. You can always submit an updated version after your due date until the last part is finished.

Shading is optional. See the Palette page for more on shading.

Frame by frame is discouraged but not forbidden.

A higher framerate is greatly recommended.

If you use a program that is designed for frame by frame (FlipAClip, TVPaint, Flash MX 2004, etc.), please consider using a different program that can produce smooth and precise tweens.

Part Formatting
  • 16x9 aspect ratio, 720p or greater: common pixel formats are 1440x720, 1920x1080, 2880x1620, and 3840x2160. The final project will be 3840x2160.
  • At least 24fps. The final project will be 60fps.
  • Try your best to match the colors as exact as possible. If they're too far off, your part will be edited.
  • No personal watermarks (they will be added to the final project)
  • No program watermarks, no exceptions
  • High quality background music is not required
  • Green/Blue screen is allowed (but stay away from red screen)
Submitting your part

Upload your final part to MyAirBridge via the 'Send Files Via Link' option (you don't need email!) and include this link in the description of your video. Do not put the link in a comment.

It is preferred that your submitted part is either exactly as long as the part shown in the call video or a bit longer, without any extra cutoffs.

The version you upload to YouTube does not need to be identical to this one. You can add cutoffs and watermarks to the version on YT if you'd like.

Palette

If you're using Sony VEGAS, please compare your final render to your preview window. If the colors in the render look off, you'll have to add to the entire project the Levels effect with the preset Computer RGB to Studio RGB. This will make your preview look off but it will correct your render.

Refer to these examples for how to color your part, but refer to the palette image to pick your colors. Screenshot the image if you can't download it or if you have difficulties opening it.

Also refer to the MAP call for more examples with different types of characters.

Various Rules
  • Black can be used as a color, white cannot
  • No gradients, except in the background
  • The background doesn't have to be a gradient, it can be abstract or defined, but it must use only shades between the two indicated colors
  • The pink outline is mandatory.
  • Yellow can also be used for accents that represent the darkness of the character, but use it sparingly
  • Shading is allowed, but optional. Shade with black and white cell shading on an Overlay layer. You can adjust the opacity of this layer but it must be Overlay, and it must be done with black and white. Do not use an airbrush, any kind of blurring, nor gradients.

Signup Details

Due Dates

Obviously, your part is due 72 hours from when you were accepted. The exact time I will be basing it on will be included next to your name in the description, and will be written in EST.

Should you not finish by the 72 hour mark, you can receive a maximum of two extensions. The length of each extension will be defined by you; if you think it'll take you one more day, you will get one more day. You won't be allowed to abuse this freedom, and you will be held accountable when time's up. Don't join if you don't think you'll finish.

In a perfect world, this MAP will be released on Valentine's Day.

Characters

A single character can only be featured once in this MAP, however, if the parts they feature in are back to back, they can be used in multiple parts. Be sure to check the description to see what characters are already being used. If you want to use a character already listed, you'll have to take an adjacent part.*

You might want to consider contacting the participant that also has your character so you can make the parts flow well.

By signing up for my MAP and providing me content, you give me full rights to edit, reuse, repost, and/or deny the usage of your work. You must trust I will credit you appropriately.
Comment Formatting

Include the codeword 'Hey' in your comment. Your comment will be disregarded if this codeword is absent.

Include the part number(s) you want. You can have up to two parts, except if you want parts 9-13, which are too long to be doubled up. Taking two parts will extend your time allotment to 144 hours, but you are expected to have one part done by 72 hours. It's greatly preferred if your parts are next to each other, but if they aren't, please provide reasoning why you want those parts specifically.

Make sure you include the character you want to use as discussed in the About slide.
